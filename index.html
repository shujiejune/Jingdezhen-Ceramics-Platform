Jingdezhen: modern ceramic art flourishing

Appreciate the four featured branches of ceramic artworks named after their decoration styles, distinguished by the glazing techniques and firing process, thriving in Jingdezhen with craftsmanship and creativity of a galaxy of contempoaray artists.

Blue-and-white

White pottery and porcelain with underglaze blue painting. In China, a most commonly used style of decoration is based on sinuous plant forms spreading across the object.

View the gallery

Famille jaune, noire, rose, verte

Porcelain categorized by the overglaze colored enamel. A range of soft color palette are used in these wares.

View the gallery

Rice-pattern

Porcelain decorated with translucent rice-shape patterns. The rice-patterns are formed by hollowing out the ware on the sidewall and filling the holes with special glaze.

View the gallery

Colored glaze

Decoration of the main body of the ware is done in color. Craftsmen compound multiple minerals into the glaze, and control the fire to lay gorgeous palette on ceramics.

View the gallery
